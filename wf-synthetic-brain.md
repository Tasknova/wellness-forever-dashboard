## 15. Additional FAQ

**Q: What's the biggest single thing we could do to improve EBITDA margin?**
A: Category mix shift. Moving 5 percentage points from Rx (20% margin) and FMCG (17.5%) into Wellness (36%) and Beauty (38.5%) would add 1.2-1.5 EBITDA percentage points. This is why the fitness corners, WF Essentials private label, and festive wellness campaigns are not just growth plays — they're the margin transformation path to IPO-ready financials.

**Q: How does our digital strategy compare to PharmEasy?**
A: We're behind on digital ordering (PharmEasy has millions of app users, we have 2,400 prescription captures in 2 months), but we're ahead on trust, compliance, and pharmacist interaction. Our strategy isn't to become PharmEasy — it's to use digital (WhatsApp prescriptions, QR scanning) to extend our physical pharmacy advantage. The regulatory environment is moving in our favor: e-pharmacy regulations may require physical verification, which benefits brick-and-mortar players like us.

**Q: What would happen if we lost both Fortis and Apollo?**
A: That's ₹21.1Cr annual revenue at risk (₹12.4Cr Apollo + ₹8.7Cr Fortis), or 2.5% of total revenue. But the institutional segment has outsized importance: it provides predictable recurring orders, anchors vendor negotiations (volume commitments), and serves as a reference for other hospital/corporate accounts. Losing both would also signal to the market that WF can't compete for institutional business — damaging the TCS expansion narrative. This scenario has <5% probability (Apollo is stable at 22% risk; Fortis at 41% is the real concern).

**Q: What's our readiness for new CDSCO cold chain regulations?**
A: 80% ready. After the Lupin audit, we invested ₹8L in temperature monitors across 14 insulin-handling stores. Remaining: ₹4L for 6 more stores + real-time logging system integration (IT project, estimated 3-week implementation). Lupin upgrading vehicles. Timeline comfortable if we start remaining work by May 15.

**Q: Which competitor should we worry about most?**
A: Apollo Pharmacy in Bengaluru and MedPlus in Pune — different threats. Apollo has brand trust, hospital network, and now corporate wellness division (direct threat to TCS account). MedPlus has aggressive pricing, rapid expansion, and a digital app showing real-time stock. In Mumbai, we're well-defended (85 stores, strong brand). Goa is safe (WF dominant). The battlegrounds are Pune (MedPlus price war) and Karnataka (late entrant vs. established Apollo).

Deepen strategic partnerships with Cipla (#1) and Sun Pharma (#2) — targeting exclusive deals and co-marketing. Grow DRL aggressively to become #3 (category captain pilot, potential dedicated supply chain manager). Fix or exit Mankind by Jun 30. Develop Zydus as a generic arbitrage play (Pantop model for more categories). Launch WF Essentials private label through Cipla/Zydus contract manufacturing to control margins. The vendor strategy directly enables the IPO margin story.
